Our Story

Two floors.
One soul.

A pub and bouchon by Dave Strauss and Henry Harris.

Three Compasses, a pub for the last 300 years, will offer a good range of beers, wines and spirits with a simple menu reflective of Bouchon Racine upstairs. You will find Bouchon Racine upstairs overlooking Cowcross Street — a French bistro in the heart of London, serving the honest, soulful dishes of the Lyonnais tradition.

Downstairs, the pub remains what it has always been: a meeting place for butchers and bankers, writers and workers, locals and travellers. Upstairs, the bouchon offers something rarer — the warm, generous cooking of a Lyonnais mother, served in a room that glows like a candle in the Farringdon evening.

Bouchon Racine

A Lyonnais bouchon above the London street.

Overlooking Cowcross Street, Bouchon Racine is our intimate dining room — a Parisian-Lyonnais bistro transplanted to Farringdon. Candlelight, vintage mirrors, banquette seating, and the smell of butter, wine and garlic. The menu is rooted in the rustic traditions of Lyon: rich, generous, and unmistakably French.

Three Compasses

A London pub, three centuries in the making.

For 300 years, the Three Compasses has stood on Cowcross Street — serving the workers of Smithfield Market, the printers of Fleet Street, and now the city that has grown around it. A good range of beers, wines and spirits, with a simple menu reflective of the bouchon upstairs. Wood panelling, cask ales, and a fire in winter.

Three Centuries

A History of Three Compasses

From Smithfield butchers to Farringdon diners — 300 years on Cowcross Street.

1724
The Doors Open
Three Compasses is licensed on Cowcross Street, serving the cattle drivers and butchers of Smithfield Market. Ale, bread, and a warm fire.
1851
A Victorian Refit
The pub is rebuilt in grand Victorian style — etched glass, mahogany bar, ornate tilework. Many of these features survive to this day.
1940
Through the Blitz
The Three Compasses endures the London Blitz, offering shelter, ale and solace to firefighters and East Enders through the longest nights.
1975
A Local Institution
Three Compasses becomes a beloved Farringdon local — known for cask ales, Sunday roasts, and the kind of publicans who remember your regular order.
2023
A New Vision
Dave Strauss and Henry Harris take the reins. The pub is lovingly restored; the upstairs dining room is reimagined as Bouchon Racine.
Today
A Pub and Bouchon
Three Compasses downstairs pours pints for the neighbourhood. Bouchon Racine upstairs serves the soul food of Lyon. Three hundred years, two floors, one heart.
